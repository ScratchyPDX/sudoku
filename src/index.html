Tips for Getting Started

How to play

Your goal is to fill every row, column, and square with the numbers 1 through 9 without repeats.

The difficulty level of the puzzle determines how many numbers are filled in at the start of the puzzle, and you'll need to use process of elimination to fill in the rest.

Most players begin one of two ways:

Look for a row, column, or square that has more numbers filled in than the others, or

Find a number that's mostly filled in and work to find the remaining placements of that number.

When in doubt, don't just guess! Even if a number works in that row, column, or square, it may lead to conflicts as you continue filling in the puzzle. It can quickly become impossible to find the source of your error and force you to start over.

Instead of guessing, use notes to help you track possible answers as you work to solve the puzzle. The "show conflicts" setting, which is on by default, works for notes and entries so you'll know if your number conflicts with an existing entry.

Saving your progress is accomplished using local storage. This means the saved puzzles are only available on the device they were saved on.
